The customer guide.

You signed your loan agreement. Welcome — you're now a customer for as long as your loan runs (often 10–20 years). This guide covers how to read your account, make payments, and handle the moments along the way: defers, extra payments, hardships, payoff.

01 · About you

Who this is for.

If you've signed a loan agreement, you're a customer. Your portal is now centered on your loan account, not your application — the application is closed and archived.

The four lifecycle states

Your portal adapts to where you are in the loan's life:

  • Current — All installments paid on time. This is where most customers spend most of their time.
  • Watch — One or two installments overdue. Your portal shows a banner; Follow-up will reach out.
  • Delinquent — Three or more installments overdue. Restrictions begin (statements held, etc).
  • Paid off — Loan fully satisfied. Portal becomes read-only and archived. Congrats.

You'll also see a brief handover banner in your first week, transitioning you from your sales agent to your follow-up agent.

03 · Flows

What you'll do.

Seven flows cover everything from a routine payment to a payoff request.

01
Make a monthly payment
2 min · Most common

Every month you'll have one installment due. You can pay early, on time, or use whatever auto-payment your org supports.

  1. Open your portal
    The next installment is the first thing you see.
  2. Click Pay now
    Pre-filled with the installment amount. You can also pay more — see flow 03 below.
  3. Choose payment method
    Saved methods are listed first. Bank transfer reference is shown if you prefer manual.
  4. Confirm
    Payment shows as Pending until Finance confirms — usually same business day.
  5. Receipt
    Receipt is emailed and added to Documents. The installment in your schedule turns green.
02
Read your schedule and history
5 min

The Schedule tab shows every installment for the life of your loan. Past installments are color-coded by status (paid, late, written off). Future ones show projected dates.

The Payments tab is the audit trail — every money movement in/out, with a receipt or document attached.

03
Pay extra (principal reduction)
3 min

Paying more than your installment amount lowers your outstanding principal and can shorten your loan. How the extra is applied depends on the org's policy — usually it reduces principal directly, sometimes it pre-pays the next installment.

Ask before you do

Some loans have prepayment penalties. Check your agreement or message your follow-up agent before paying down a big chunk.

04
Request a deferral
10 min · For hardship

If you can't make one specific payment, ask for a deferral. It moves that installment's due date forward — usually by 30 or 60 days. Late fees may or may not apply depending on the reason.

  1. Message your follow-up agent
    Explain the situation. The earlier the better — calling before the due date avoids late fees.
  2. Agent files a deferral request
    Finance reviews and approves (or declines) within 1–2 business days.
  3. Sign the deferral letter
    A short document arrives in your portal for e-signature.
  4. New due date
    Your schedule updates with the new date — future installments are unchanged.
05
Request a payoff quote
2 min

Want to pay off the whole loan? Request a payoff quote — a formal statement of the exact amount owed today, including any accrued interest. Quotes are valid for a defined window (typically 10–14 days).

  1. Open your account kebab menu
    Click Request payoff quote.
  2. Choose effective date
    Today or up to 14 days out.
  3. Receive PDF quote
    Emailed and added to Documents. Wire instructions included.
  4. Send wire by deadline
    Once received, loan closes and your portal goes to Paid off.
06
Dispute a charge or fee
15 min

If something looks wrong — a late fee you don't recognize, a payment that didn't post — open a dispute. Finance and Follow-up will investigate together.

07
Download statements and receipts
1 min

Monthly statements are auto-generated and stored in Documents. You can also generate an ad-hoc statement (annual summary, year-to-date) from the account kebab menu.

04 · Monthly rhythm

What to expect each month.

  • Day −7 — Email reminder of upcoming installment.
  • Day −2 — SMS reminder.
  • Due date — Auto-debit runs (if set up), or pay manually.
  • Day +1 — If unpaid, late status begins; an email + SMS goes out.
  • Day +5 — Late fee assessed per your agreement.
  • Day +15 — Follow-up agent calls you.
  • Month-end — Statement generated and emailed.

08 · FAQ

Common questions.

What happens if I miss a payment?

Day +1 you get an email and SMS. Day +5 a late fee is assessed. Day +15 your follow-up agent calls. Don't wait — the earlier you reach out about a problem, the more flexibility you have.

Can I change my due date?

Yes, with manager approval. Message your follow-up agent. Usually allowed once per year.

How do I update my bank account for auto-debit?

Account → Payment methods → Edit. Verification takes 1–2 business days; the old method stays active until verified.

What if I want to sell the property?

The loan stays with you unless it's explicitly transferable. Talk to your follow-up agent — payoff at sale is the most common path.

What if my partner and I want to add them to the loan?

Possible but requires a full restructure with both parties. The Org admin will assign a senior agent to walk you through it.

Why is my balance not going down faster?

Early in a loan, most of each installment goes to interest. Check your schedule — the principal portion grows each month. Paying extra accelerates this.

Can I pay in cash?

Some orgs accept cash at office locations. Check Account → Payment methods or ask your agent.

What if I get a fraud alert?

Don't click any link in suspicious emails. Log in to your portal directly via the URL you know, and check Activity. Genuine messages from the org always appear in your Messages tab too.
