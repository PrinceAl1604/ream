Finance line · Internal

Finance operator manual.

Finance owns the loan ledger after signing — every dollar in and out, every installment posted, every aging bucket. This manual covers all three tiers and the daily / monthly flows: confirming payments, reconciling against the bank, running aging, generating payoff quotes, closing the period.

01 · About the role

Who Finance is, and what each tier owns.

Finance is the second of the three internal lines. The single defining rule: nothing posts to a loan ledger without a Finance action. Payments enter a review queue first; an agent (or above) confirms and allocates.

Agent

Finance agent

Works the payment review queue. Confirms incoming payments, allocates them across installments / fees / principal, posts receipts. Investigates NSFs and bounced auto-debits. Answers payment-posting questions routed from Follow-up.

Owns: the day-to-day queue. Sees: all loan accounts, all transactions; can post and allocate but cannot write off or restructure.

Manager

Finance manager

Owns aging, reconciliation, and approvals. Runs the bank-statement reconciliation, signs off on write-offs, approves payoff quotes and deferrals. Coaches finance agents on allocation rules.

Owns: the ledger's health. Sees: everything an agent sees + aging report + reconciliation tools + approval queues.

Admin

Finance admin

Owns the financial configuration: loan products (term, interest, deposit, fees), interest models, fee policies, the period-close mechanism, write-off rules, payoff quote logic. Coordinates with Org admin on chart of accounts.

Owns: the function. Sees: everything + configuration. Cannot create users (Org admin) or build offers (Sales).

03 · Daily flows

What Finance does, end-to-end.

Seven flows cover daily queue work through monthly period close.

01
Confirm a payment (Agent)
Daily · 2 min each

The default work of a Finance agent. Payments arrive automatically from bank feeds, card processors, and manual entries. Your job: identify the loan, allocate the money, post.

  1. Open Payment review
    Sidebar → Payment review. New payments highlighted.
  2. Verify the suggested match
    REAM matches by reference, amount, and customer name. High confidence (> 90%) → confirm with one click. Lower → manual.
  3. Allocate
    Default: oldest installment first, then late fees, then principal. Override if instructed by customer note.
  4. Post
    Hits the ledger. Customer's portal updates within seconds; receipt emails.
Don't guess on ambiguous matches

If you're not sure which loan a payment belongs to, leave it in the queue with a note and route to the manager. Mis-allocation is much harder to undo than to defer.

02
Handle a bounced / NSF payment
Daily · 5 min

Bank feed posts a "returned" entry. The original posting is auto-reversed; the loan goes back to its prior state; an NSF fee applies per loan-product config.

  1. Open the Reversals queue
    Auto-collected. Each row links back to the original posting.
  2. Verify reason
    NSF, account-closed, payment-stopped — each triggers different downstream behavior.
  3. Add internal note & notify Follow-up
    Loop the follow-up agent so they can call the customer same-day.
  4. Confirm fee posting
    Some orgs auto-assess NSF fees; some require Finance action.
03
Run reconciliation (Manager)
Weekly · 30 min

Match the loan ledger against the bank statement. REAM auto-matches by reference and amount; unmatched items go to a worklist.

  1. Reports → Reconciliation
    Upload or auto-fetch the bank statement.
  2. Review auto-matches
    Sample a few; the rest are bulk-confirmed.
  3. Investigate unmatched
    Bank entries without a ledger record (could be a misposted incoming payment) and ledger records without a bank entry (could be a pending or fraudulent posting).
  4. Close the rec
    Click Mark reconciled. Locks the period for that bank account.
04
Review the aging report (Manager)
Weekly · 20 min

Aging buckets all overdue installments by days past due: 1–30, 31–60, 61–90, 91+. Each bucket triggers different escalation in Follow-up.

  1. Reports → Aging
    Defaults to current as-of date.
  2. Look at the 31+ buckets
    These are the actionable ones. Verify Follow-up is engaged on each.
  3. Flag for write-off review
    91+ rows that have had no contact in 30 days → manager review for charge-off.
05
Generate a payoff quote
15 min

Customer requests one through their portal; it arrives in your queue. You set the effective date and the system computes principal + accrued interest + fees as of that date.

  1. Open the loan detail
    From the payoff request notification.
  2. Click Payoff calculator
    Set effective date (today through +14 days).
  3. Review and approve (Manager)
    Tier check: agents can compute, managers approve.
  4. Send PDF quote
    Auto-emailed to customer with wire instructions. Logged on timeline.
06
Approve a write-off (Admin)
30 min · Rare

When a loan can't be collected and Follow-up has exhausted contact attempts. Admin moves the loan to write-off status; ledger keeps the audit trail.

  1. Loan detail → Actions → Write off
    Requires reason code and supporting notes.
  2. Choose write-off amount
    Full balance, principal only, or fees only.
  3. Confirm
    Loan moves to Charged-off. Customer portal becomes read-only with a "Closed" banner.
07
Close the period (Admin)
Monthly · 2 hours

End-of-month: lock the period, run statements, deliver investor / leadership reports. Once closed, nothing in the period can be edited without an admin reversal.

  1. Verify reconciliation is complete
    Every bank account marked reconciled for the month.
  2. Run pre-close report
    Validates no orphans, no unposted payments, no broken allocations.
  3. Lock the period
    Admin action. Stamps every transaction.
  4. Generate statements
    Customer statements emailed; portfolio summary to leadership; investor report exported.
04 · Period close

Monthly rhythm.

  • Daily (Agent) — Clear payment review queue. Target: zero items older than 24h.
  • Daily (Agent) — Process reversals; loop in Follow-up.
  • Weekly (Manager) — Reconciliation. Aging review. Approvals queue.
  • Mid-month (Admin) — Interim portfolio report to leadership.
  • Month-end (Admin) — Period close: rec → lock → statements → reports.
  • Quarterly (Admin) — Loan-product review with Sales admin. Fee policy review.
  • Annually (Admin) — Interest model review. Charge-off policy review with Org admin.

08 · FAQ & gotchas

Things that trip people up.

A customer says they paid but I don't see it.

Three places to check: (1) the review queue — maybe waiting on confirm. (2) the reversals queue — could have bounced. (3) the bank feed — could be a delay between bank and REAM (usually < 24h). If still nothing, ask the customer for their bank confirmation reference and search payments by ref.

I allocated a payment incorrectly. How do I fix it?

Agents can't reverse — escalate to a manager. The manager reverses the posting (audited) and you re-allocate from the queue.

The bank statement and ledger disagree by a small amount.

Usually a fee posting timing mismatch. Reconciliation will let you mark it as "Pending — fee timing" and clear it the following period when both sides line up.

Can I edit a loan after it's booked?

Not the contract terms — those are fixed by signature. You can run a restructure (which creates a new schedule), apply a deferral, or assess/waive fees. Anything that changes principal/term requires manager+admin sign-off.

When does interest accrue?

Depends on the loan product. Most are monthly accrual on the due date; some are daily. Check the loan product config — Finance admin owns this.

How do I handle a partial payment?

Allocate to the oldest installment first; what's left rolls. Some orgs prefer to bounce partials back; check your allocation policy. Customer's portal shows their installment as "Partially paid".

A payment came in for a closed/paid-off loan.

Don't post to the closed account. Park in suspense, contact Follow-up to confirm intent, and either refund or apply to another loan the customer has.

What if the period needs to be re-opened?

Admin can reverse the close. It's audited and rare — usually only for found-money or auditor adjustments. Statements regenerate.
