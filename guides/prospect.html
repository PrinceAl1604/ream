The prospect guide.

You submitted an interest form — congrats, you're in motion. This guide walks through the portal you now have access to, what your sales agent will ask of you, and how to move toward an offer and signing.

01 · About you

Who this is for.

If you've submitted an interest form and gotten a portal magic link, you're a prospect. Your status lasts from interest submission until you either sign a loan agreement (becoming a Customer) or withdraw.

What changes from being a visitor

  • You now have a portal with a personalized view of your application progress.
  • A specific sales agent is assigned to your case — your first point of contact for everything.
  • You can upload documents, schedule site visits, and see real offers when they're ready.
  • You'll get email and SMS updates when something needs your attention.
When you become a customer

The moment you sign a loan agreement, your portal transforms. The application disappears and your loan account takes its place. See the customer guide for what comes next.

03 · Flows

What you'll do.

Seven flows cover everything a prospect needs to do. The big ones are uploading documents and reviewing your offer.

01
Upload a requested document
3 min

Your sales agent will create document requests — usually for ID, proof of income, and address. Each request has a name, instructions, and an upload box.

  1. Request details — what they need and why.
  2. Upload area — drag-and-drop or click to pick a file. Photos of paper docs are fine.
  3. Submit — once submitted, the request is locked. Reach out if you uploaded the wrong file.
02
Schedule a site visit
2 min

Site visits are scheduled either by your agent or directly by you from the Schedule tab. You'll see available slots and can book or reschedule.

  1. Open Schedule
    From the sidebar in your application portal.
  2. Pick a date
    Open slots are highlighted. You can also propose a custom time in the notes.
  3. Confirm
    You'll get a calendar invite and SMS reminder the day before.
03
Message your sales agent
1 min

Use the Messages tab in your portal rather than personal email — it keeps the conversation in your case file and makes sure the right people see it.

Review and accept an offer
10 min · The big moment

Once Finance reviews your application, your agent will issue a formal offer. This is the real (non-indicative) financing proposal — read it carefully.

  3. Accept & sign — opens the loan agreement for e-signature. Once signed, you're a customer.
Take your time

Offers stay valid for a defined window (typically 7–14 days). Don't feel pressured. If you want to negotiate, message your agent before accepting.

05
Add documents proactively
1 min

You don't have to wait for a document request. If you have something useful (employment letter, bank statements showing savings), upload it under Documents → Add document.

06
Update your contact info
1 min

Phone or email changed? Update them in Account → Profile. SMS reminders and offer notifications go to whatever's on file.

07
Withdraw an application
2 min

If you change your mind, open the Application page and click Withdraw. Your agent will confirm — and your case is closed cleanly. No money is owed.

04 · While you wait

Between steps.

Most of being a prospect is waiting for the next step. Here's what's happening behind the scenes and what to look out for.

Financial review (1–2 weeks)

After you submit all required documents, Finance reviews your file. They may come back with follow-up questions — your agent will relay them. Average time-to-offer is around 10 business days.

Site visits (whenever you want)

You don't have to visit a property to apply, but most prospects do. Book one before or after the offer.

Notifications

You'll get an email or SMS when: a new document request is created, an offer is issued, or a site visit is scheduled. Check the portal anytime — it's always current.

08 · FAQ

Common questions.

What if my magic link expired?

Go to the portal sign-in page and request a new link. It arrives within a minute.

How long does the application take?

Typically 2–4 weeks from interest submission to signed agreement. The biggest variable is how quickly you can provide requested documents.

Can I apply for more than one property?

Yes — each becomes a separate application. They show up as separate cards in your portal.

Why is Finance asking for more documents?

Finance reviews each file individually. Common follow-ups include bank statements, employment verification, or proof of additional income. Reply through your agent.

What does "indicative" mean vs a real offer?

Indicative payments on the public site are based on the property price and standard terms. A real offer is calibrated to your specific financial profile after Finance review.

Can I negotiate the offer?

Sometimes — message your agent before accepting. The down payment and term are most commonly negotiable; the interest rate usually isn't.

What if I want to switch properties?

Tell your agent. They can either re-target your existing application or open a new one for the new property.
