Sales operator manual.

Sales owns the relationship from first inbound interest through signed contract. This manual covers every tier — agent, manager, admin — and the daily, weekly, and monthly flows each one runs. When the contract is signed and the loan is booked, Sales hands the customer over to Follow-up.

01 · About the role

Who Sales is, and what each tier owns.

Sales is one of three internal lines. Every Sales user has a tier — Agent, Manager, or Admin — that scopes what they see and can change. Tiers are stackable in spirit: a manager sees everything an agent sees plus their team's view; an admin sees the whole org plus configuration.

Agent

Sales agent

Owns a portfolio of prospects. Works through today's tasks, hot leads, and quota progress. Logs every call, email, and visit on the prospect timeline. Builds offers from loan products. Walks signing day with the borrower.

Owns: their portfolio. Sees: their pipeline, their dashboard, the inventory catalog, document templates, their own activity history.

Manager

Sales manager

Runs a team of agents. Sees the whole team's funnel, leaderboard, forecast, and coaching queue. Reassigns prospects when an agent is overloaded or on leave. Approves offer exceptions outside policy.

Owns: the team's number. Sees: everything an agent sees + team rollup + agent-level breakdowns + reassignment tools + coaching notes.

Admin

Sales admin

Owns the whole sales surface — pipeline org-wide, lead-source mix, site demand, revenue-to-plan. Sets sales policies and templates. Defines stages, quotas, and routing rules.

Owns: the function. Sees: all teams, all pipelines, all configuration. Cannot edit the loan ledger (that's Finance) or invite users (that's Org admin).

03 · Daily flows

What Sales does, end-to-end.

Seven flows cover the full lifecycle from a new inbound prospect to a signed contract and handover to Follow-up.

01
Work the pipeline (Agent)
Daily · 30 min

The pipeline is your inbox. Open the Prospects list with the default "My pipeline" filter applied. Sort by stage, last activity, or due-date.

  1. Open Prospects
    Sidebar → Prospects. Filter chips at top: Stage, Owner: me, Hot, Due today.
  2. Clear the "Due today" queue
    Every prospect with a follow-up scheduled today shows here. Either complete the activity or reschedule with a reason.
  3. Triage new inbound
    Anything in the "New" stage with no owner — claim it or let Routing reassign.
  4. Move stale prospects
    Any prospect with no activity in 14 days needs a touch or a "Lost — reason" disposition.
02
Log an activity
Every interaction · 1 min

Everything goes on the timeline — calls, "no answer", emails, SMS, WhatsApp, notes. Sub-activities let you chain a follow-up call to the original email so the thread is visible in one place.

  1. Open the prospect's detail page
    Click their name from any list. The timeline is on the right.
  2. Click + Activity
    Pick a type (call, email, SMS, note, visit). Activity templates pre-fill body.
  3. Set parent (optional)
    For follow-up calls, set "Sub-activity of…" pointing to the original email. The two now link both ways.
  4. Save
    It appears on the timeline immediately. Outcome ("Connected", "No answer", etc) is required for calls.
03
Run a site visit
2–3 hours · Field

Site visits are the single highest-converting touchpoint. Schedule one as soon as the prospect is qualified.

  1. Schedule it
    From the prospect detail page → Activities → New site visit. Pick the site, time, attendees.
  2. Send confirmation
    Auto-email with location pin and your photo. SMS reminder 24h prior.
  3. On-site: log notes from mobile
    Open the prospect on phone. Quick voice-to-text notes; attach plot photos.
  4. Disposition
    Within 2 hours of leaving: Interested / Maybe / Lost. This auto-advances the stage.
  5. Schedule next touch
    Always leave the visit with a follow-up scheduled — never with "I'll get back to them".
04
Build an offer
10 min · After site visit

Offers are computed from loan products — pre-defined templates with term, interest, deposit, and fee structure. The agent picks a product, customizes deposit and term, and the system computes the monthly installment.

app.ream.com/prospects/p123/offerOpen in app
The offer builder. Loan product on the left; computed terms on the right.
  1. Open the prospect → Offer tab
    Or click "Build offer" from the timeline.
  2. Pick the loan product
    Most orgs have 3–6. Standard / Premium / Land-only.
  3. Pick the property
    Search the inventory catalog. Price flows in automatically.
  4. Tune deposit and term
    Monthly installment recomputes live.
  5. Send to prospect
    PDF generated; emailed automatically; logged as an activity.
Outside policy?

If the offer exceeds the agent-tier guardrails (deposit too low, term too long, interest discount), it goes to manager approval before it can be sent.

05
Request documents
5 min

To qualify a prospect for a loan, you need their ID, proof of income, bank statements, and any property-specific docs. Use Document requests — never email asks separately; they get lost.

  1. Prospect → Documents tab
    Click Request documents. Pick from the standard bundle or add line items.
  2. Send
    Prospect gets an email + portal alert with the checklist.
  3. Track status
    Each line shows: Requested → Uploaded → Verified. You verify (or send back).
06
Signing day & handover to Follow-up
90 min · The moment

Signing converts a prospect into a customer. The application closes, a loan account opens, and the customer enters their first lifecycle state — usually Current. Sales walks them through it, then formally hands them to a follow-up agent.

  1. Generate the contract
    From the offer page → "Generate contract". Document template fills in; review before sending.
  2. Schedule signing
    In-person or remote. Send a calendar invite.
  3. Capture signature
    Wet signature or e-sign. Confirm IDs match.
  4. Book the loan
    Click "Book loan" on the prospect detail. Application closes; loan account opens.
  5. Assign follow-up agent
    Pick from the team or accept the auto-suggestion. Send handover summary.
Don't skip the handover summary

Three lines on who this person is, what closed the deal, and what they're sensitive about. Saves the follow-up agent a week of pattern-matching.

07
Coach an agent (Manager)
Weekly · 30 min per agent

Open your team rollup, drill into an agent. Coaching queue surfaces prospects stuck > 14 days, calls with negative outcomes, or offers ignored by prospects.

  1. Dashboard → Team
    Pick the agent.
  2. Coaching queue
    Listen to call recordings (if enabled), review timelines, leave a coaching note.
  3. Reassign if blocked
    Move prospects whose agent has gone cold. Two-click action.
04 · Cadence

Weekly and monthly rhythm.

  • Daily (Agent) — Pipeline review, "due today" queue, log activities as they happen.
  • Tuesday (Manager) — Team standup. Walk the funnel. Identify stuck deals.
  • Weekly (Manager) — 1:1 coaching with each agent. 30 min.
  • Friday (Manager) — Reforecast for the month. Update commitments.
  • Monthly (Admin) — Pipeline review with leadership. Lead-source mix. Site-by-site demand.
  • Monthly (Admin) — Template hygiene: archive stale templates, write new ones for repeat asks.
  • Quarterly (Admin) — Quota recalibration. Loan-product review with Finance admin.

08 · FAQ & gotchas

Things that trip people up.

A prospect is asking me about their loan account — but they haven't signed.

Until they sign, they don't have a loan account — only an application. Use the offer page to walk through projected terms. The "Loan account" only exists after Book loan runs.

Why can't I see another agent's prospects?

By default, agents see their own portfolio only. Managers see their team. If you need cross-team visibility, ask your Sales admin to enable a temporary view.

My offer is getting flagged for manager approval. Why?

Either your tier doesn't have authority for that deposit/term combination, or there's a discount on interest. Check the warning banner on the offer page — it tells you which guardrail fired.

Can I send a contract before the prospect has uploaded docs?

You can generate one — but signing is blocked until every required document has status Verified. Your Documents tab tells you what's outstanding.

I booked a loan but the customer doesn't see their portal.

Two reasons. (a) The account-creation email may be in spam — resend from the customer detail page. (b) If they were a prospect with a portal, it switches modes; old links still work.

What if the prospect wants two properties?

One application per loan. If they want two loans, duplicate the prospect for property #2 — link them with the "Related prospects" field.

An agent left. What happens to their portfolio?

Manager (or admin) reassigns in bulk — Prospects → filter Owner: [name] → select all → Reassign. Activities and history travel with the prospect.
