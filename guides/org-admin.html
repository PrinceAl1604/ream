Organization admin · Tenant owner

Org admin operator manual.

You own the REAM tenant. You're the person who set up the org, who invites every internal user, who owns billing, and who makes policy calls that span Sales, Finance, and Follow-up. There's usually only one or two of you. This manual covers the day-one setup, the ongoing administrative rhythm, and the cross-line moments where Org admin is the only person who can act.

01 · About the role

Who Org admin is.

Org admin is the only role that's not part of a line. You sit above Sales / Finance / Follow-up and own the things that cross them: people, policy, branding, billing, integrations, audit. You don't run a pipeline, work the payment queue, or handle hardship calls. You make sure the people who do can.

Owns

People

Every internal user is invited by you. Every role/tier change is yours. When someone leaves, you offboard.

Owns

Branding & identity

Logo, colors, domain, email sender, public listings styling. Anything a customer or prospect sees as "from the org".

Owns

Billing & subscription

The REAM contract, seats, plan, payment method, invoices.

Owns

Cross-line policy

When Sales, Finance, and Follow-up disagree, you call it. Coordinate them on loan products, deferral thresholds, contract terms.

Owns

Audit & legal

Audit log, data export, GDPR/CCPA requests, legal flags on customer accounts (divorce, death, ownership transfer).

Owns

Imports & integrations

Migrating from a spreadsheet or another CRM. Bank-feed integrations, e-sign provider, email/SMS providers.

02 · Onboarding the org

Your first week setting up REAM.

A complete REAM setup is usually a single afternoon for an existing organization, plus a few days of inventory and template work. The order below matters — each step unblocks the next.

  1. Complete org profile
    Legal name, address, jurisdiction, fiscal year start, currency. This becomes the namespace for every document and report.
  2. Brand the tenant
    Logo, primary color, custom domain (or use the default subdomain), email sender, default footer text.
  3. Invite the team
    Sales admin first. Finance admin next. Follow-up admin third. Each tier admin then helps you invite their own line.
  4. Configure templates
    Loan agreement, deferral letter, payoff quote, satisfaction survey, monthly statement. Use the defaults and customize.
  5. Connect integrations
    Bank feed, e-sign provider, email/SMS provider, Google Workspace if SSO needed.
  6. Migrate data (if applicable)
    Use Imports → CSV for prospects, customers, properties, historic transactions.
  7. Publish public site
    Point your domain at the listings page; verify the public intake form posts to your tenant.
Phase the work

Day 1 is profile, branding, and one of each admin. Day 2–3 is templates and policies. Day 4+ is migration. Sites and listings can grow gradually — you don't need a full catalog to be operational.

03 · Daily & weekly flows

What Org admin does, end-to-end.

Seven flows cover the ongoing rhythm — most are sporadic, not daily. The job is more steward than operator.

01
Invite a new internal user
5 min · As needed

Every internal user is invited by you. Self-signup is not allowed for the internal app.

  1. Admin → Users → Invite
    Or the gear icon → Users.
  2. Fill name + email
    Plus role (Sales / Finance / Follow-up), tier (Agent / Manager / Admin), team if applicable.
  3. Send invite
    Emailed link, valid 7 days. They confirm and pick a password.
  4. Confirm dashboard
    After they log in, ask them to confirm what they see matches their tier. If not, adjust.
02
Offboard a leaving user
15 min · As needed

When someone leaves, you reassign their portfolio, deactivate their account, and preserve their audit trail.

  1. Reassign first, deactivate after
    Open Prospects (or Customers) → filter Owner: [name] → bulk reassign. Same for activities, document requests.
  2. Deactivate the user
    Admin → Users → [name] → Deactivate. Their login is revoked immediately.
  3. Preserve audit
    Historical activities they logged stay on timelines. The audit log keeps their login history.
  4. Notify cross-line
    Tell Sales/Finance/Follow-up admins about the change.
03
Run a quarterly audit-log review
45 min · Quarterly

The audit log records every consequential action: role changes, ledger reversals, write-offs, configuration changes, impersonations. Read through it.

  1. Admin → Audit log
    Filter by date range (last quarter).
  2. Sample by severity
    Read every impersonate, every write-off, every policy-change.
  3. Investigate anomalies
    Anything that looks unusual — DM the actor, get context.
  4. Export for compliance
    CSV export for the legal team or auditor.
04
Handle a legal flag on a customer
30 min · Rare

Divorce, death, transfer of ownership — situations where the loan can't be modified unilaterally. You set a legal flag on the customer account that locks down certain actions until resolved.

  1. Open customer detail → Actions → Set legal flag
    Pick the flag type and reason.
  2. Lock down actions
    No deferrals, no restructures, no transfers without your manual approval.
  3. Loop legal counsel
    Document everything. Outside REAM if needed.
  4. Resolve and unflag
    Once the legal matter clears, unflag the account.
05
Coordinate cross-line policy change
2 hours · Quarterly

When the org changes how it does business — new loan product, new deferral threshold, new fee schedule — multiple admins need to align.

  1. Convene the three line admins
    Sales, Finance, Follow-up.
  2. Agree the policy
    Document in plain English first; configuration follows.
  3. Update each line's config
    Loan product (Finance), pipeline / offer guardrails (Sales), cadence (Follow-up).
  4. Announce internally
    Send broadcast to the org. Note the effective date.
  5. Update templates
    Anything customer-facing that referenced the old policy.
06
Manage billing & seats
10 min · Monthly

REAM bills by seat. You monitor seat usage and pay the invoice. Plan changes happen here too.

  1. Org settings → Billing
    Current plan, seat count, invoice history.
  2. Upgrade / downgrade plan
    Self-service.
  3. Update payment method
    Card or ACH.
  4. Download invoices
    For your books.
07
Run an import / migration
2–8 hours · Rare

Bringing data in from a spreadsheet or another CRM. REAM supports CSV/Excel for prospects, customers, properties, and historic transactions.

  1. Imports → New import
    Pick what you're importing.
  2. Upload file
    CSV or Excel.
  3. Map columns
    REAM auto-detects; you correct.
  4. Preview
    First 50 rows shown. Catch obvious errors here.
  5. Run import
    Background job. Status banner on dashboard.
  6. Reconcile
    After import, spot-check 10–20 random records.
Historic transactions are tricky

Don't import historical loan transactions blindly. Coordinate with Finance admin so accruals and statements regenerate correctly. Often best done in a fresh sandbox tenant first.

04 · Quarterly & annual

The rhythm.

  • Monthly — Billing review. Seat count. Invoice download.
  • Quarterly — Audit log review. Inactive-user sweep. Template hygiene.
  • Quarterly — Cross-line policy meeting with Sales/Finance/Follow-up admins.
  • Annually — REAM contract review. Plan tier. Seat right-sizing.
  • Annually — Org branding refresh (logo, colors, footer).
  • Annually — GDPR / data retention review.
  • Ad hoc — Onboarding new users, offboarding leavers, legal flags, support escalations.

06 · Permissions

08 · FAQ & gotchas

Things that trip people up.

I want to invite someone but I'm not sure which tier.

When in doubt, invite as the lower tier — easier to promote than demote. Ask the line admin who they should report to; that usually reveals the right tier.

Can I have two Org admins?

Yes, and it's recommended for continuity. Two is the practical max — more and accountability blurs.

An admin asked me to give them ledger access. Should I?

Probably not. Finance owns the ledger by design. If they're trying to do something specific (run a report, troubleshoot an allocation), get them to do it through Finance. If Finance is unresponsive, escalate inside Finance, don't route around them.

A customer is asking to take over a deceased borrower's loan.

Set the Death legal flag immediately. Loop legal counsel. The path is usually either payoff from the estate or assumption — both require formal documentation outside REAM. Don't let any Follow-up action proceed until cleared.

How do I do a GDPR/CCPA delete request?

Org settings → Privacy → Subject request. The system anonymizes the user's personal data while preserving the audit trail of financial transactions (required by law). Customer-facing portals go dark for that user.

I accidentally invited the wrong person.

Pending invites can be revoked from the Invites page. If they've already accepted, deactivate the user and reassign their (empty) portfolio.

I forgot what was changed in our last policy update.

Audit log is your friend. Filter by policy-change events; each entry has before/after values.

REAM is broken and I'm losing business.

Status page first (status.ream.com). If down, your team's emails confirm. If up, support@ream.com with "P1 outage" in the subject — 1h SLA. Have the audit log timestamp of the first failure ready.
