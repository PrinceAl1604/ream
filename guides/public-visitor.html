The visitor guide.

You're here because you saw a property — maybe on a friend's recommendation, maybe through search — and want to learn more. No login required. This guide shows you what's available and how to take the next step when you're ready.

01 · About you

Who this guide is for.

If you can read this without being logged in — and you haven't filled out an application yet — you're a public visitor. This is the most common entry point into REAM.

What you can do without signing up

  • Browse all public sites, lands, and houses an organization has listed.
  • Compare properties side-by-side — square footage, financing options, location.
  • See pricing and indicative monthly payments for each property.
  • Submit interest to open a conversation — this is the only action that creates an account.

When you stop being a visitor

The moment you submit an interest form, you become a Prospect — and you'll get an email with portal access. From there, your guide is the prospect guide.

03 · Flows

What you can do.

Five things you can accomplish without an account. Most visitors will only do the first one or two.

01
Browse listings and compare properties
3 min

Use the catalog to filter down to properties you actually want to consider. Save links you like — there's no favorites button without an account.

  1. Go to the catalog
    Either click a friend's link or visit the listings page directly.
  2. Filter
    Use the toolbar to filter by type, price range, location, or amenities.
  3. Compare
    Open listings in multiple browser tabs to compare. Each card shows the headline numbers.
  4. Bookmark in your browser
    There's no "favorites" without an account — use browser bookmarks for now.
02
Read a property detail page
5 min

Every property has a public detail page with photos, key specs, financing options, and a contact CTA. This is where you'll spend most of your time.

  1. Gallery — photos, video tour, and floor plans if available.
  2. Indicative payment — what monthly payments look like at common down-payment levels.
  3. I'm interested button — this is the only action that creates an account.
03
Submit an interest form
2 min · Creates an account

This is the moment you stop being a visitor and become a Prospect. The form is short — basic contact info and a few details about your situation — and it takes about two minutes.

  1. Click I'm interested
    From any property detail page. A short form opens inline.
  2. Enter contact info
    Name, phone, email. The phone number is how Sales will reach you first.
  3. Answer screening questions
    Roughly 4 to 6 questions — household size, employment, intended use. Different orgs ask different things.
  4. Submit
    You'll see a confirmation page and get an email with a portal magic link within a minute.
  5. Check your email
    The magic link logs you into your prospect portal with no password setup.
What happens next

Within one business day, a sales agent will call you to confirm interest, answer questions, and schedule a site visit if you want one. You don't need to do anything until then.

04
Use the indicative payment calculator
1 min

Every property detail page has a small calculator that shows estimated monthly payments at common down-payment levels. These are indicative only — your actual offer depends on financial review.

  1. Scroll to the financing section
    It's usually right below the gallery on a property page.
  2. Pick a down-payment percentage
    Common options are 10%, 20%, 30%. Some orgs allow 5%.
  3. Pick a term
    Most orgs offer 5, 10, 15, or 20-year terms. Longer term = lower monthly, higher total cost.
  4. Note the result
    Indicative monthly payment. Real numbers come after you apply and Finance reviews.
05
Contact the organization without applying
1 min

If you have questions but aren't ready to submit an interest form, every public site has a contact section in the footer — phone, email, sometimes WhatsApp. You can also visit physical offices listed on the About page.

04 · Over time

Coming back later.

A property purchase is a long decision. Most visitors come back multiple times before applying. Here's what tends to happen between visits.

New listings appear

Organizations add inventory regularly. If you're shopping over weeks or months, check the catalog — there may be new options.

Listings can disappear

When a property is sold or reserved, it's removed from the public catalog. If a property you liked is gone, contact the org — sometimes there are similar units coming up.

Prices can change

Pricing on the public site is current at the moment you see it. If you bookmarked a property and come back later, the price may have updated. The indicative payment calculator always uses the current price.

07 · Glossary

What everything means.

The full REAM vocabulary — the same glossary appears in every role's guide so terms always match.

Public visitor Role
Anyone browsing the public site without an account. No saved state.
Prospect Role
Someone who submitted an interest form. Has a portal scoped to the application until signing.
Customer Role
A prospect who has signed a loan agreement. Has the full customer portal.
Sales Role
Internal team that owns the relationship from interest to signing.
Finance Role
Internal team that owns the loan ledger after signing.
Follow-up Role
Internal team that owns the post-signing customer relationship.
Organization admin Role
Owner of the REAM tenant. Manages internal users and policies.
Listing Inventory
A property published on the public catalog — a site, a land, or a house.
Site Inventory
A development project containing many individual plots and/or houses.
Land Inventory
An individual plot of land within a site or sold standalone.
House Inventory
A built or to-be-built dwelling on a plot.
Interest form Inventory
The short application that converts a visitor into a prospect.
Application Lifecycle
The case file tracking a prospect from interest to signing.
Offer Lifecycle
A real (non-indicative) financing proposal sent to a prospect after financial review.
Site visit Lifecycle
A scheduled in-person tour of a property arranged by a sales agent.
Closing Lifecycle
The signing event that converts a prospect into a customer.
Indicative payment Financial
Estimated monthly payment shown on the public site. Not a quote — actual numbers come from Finance.
Down payment Financial
The cash portion paid upfront. Common values are 10%, 20%, 30%.
Term Financial
How long the loan runs — typically 5, 10, 15, or 20 years.
Principal Financial
The amount you actually owe, separate from interest.
Interest Financial
The cost of borrowing, calculated on the outstanding principal.
Installment Financial
One scheduled monthly payment containing principal + interest.
Late fee Financial
An additional charge when an installment is paid past its due date.
Defer Financial
To move a single installment's due date forward, by agreement.
Restructure Financial
To recompute the entire schedule — usually after hardship.
Magic link Access
An emailed link that logs you into your portal without a password.
Portal Access
Your personal logged-in area — different for prospects and customers.
Document request Access
A task asking you to upload a specific document (ID, payslip, etc).
Statement Access
A formal PDF summary of your loan or payments. Customers only.
08 · FAQ

Common questions.

Do I need an account to look at listings?

No. Everything on the public catalog is visible without logging in. An account is only created when you submit an interest form.

Can I save favorites without signing up?

Not in REAM directly. Use your browser's bookmarks or the share button to save links. Once you submit interest, you'll be able to track properties in your prospect portal.

Are the prices on the public site final?

Property prices are current at the time you see them, but pricing can change. The monthly payment shown is indicative — your actual financing offer depends on Finance review.

How long after I apply will someone contact me?

Within one business day in most cases. A sales agent will call the number on your interest form to introduce themselves and answer questions.

Will my data be shared?

Only with the organization you applied to. See the privacy policy for full details on storage, retention, and your rights.

What if I change my mind after submitting?

Reply to the welcome email and tell the agent you're no longer interested. Your application will be marked closed — no obligation.
