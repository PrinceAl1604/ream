1.Information About Brokerage Services (IABS)

Texas law requires all real-estate license holders to give the following information about brokerage services to prospective buyers, tenants, sellers, and landlords:

  • A broker is responsible for all brokerage activities, including acts performed by sales agents sponsored by the broker.
  • A sales agent must be sponsored by a broker and works with clients on behalf of the broker.
  • A license holder can represent a party in a real-estate transaction as an exclusive agent, an intermediary, or a subagent.

For questions, contact the Texas Real Estate Commission at 1700 N. Congress Ave., Austin, TX 78701 or www.trec.texas.gov. The IABS form (TXR-2501) is available on request.

2.Texas Property Code Subchapter D — Executory Contracts

Owner-financed real-estate sales in Texas are governed by Texas Property Code Chapter 5, Subchapter D. Under this subchapter, Ream must, at least 7 calendar days before signing:

  • Provide the buyer with a complete tax certificate showing all current property taxes due.
  • Provide a copy of the deed restrictions, if any.
  • Provide notice of any liens, encumbrances, or other claims on the property.
  • Provide an annual accounting statement to each buyer by January 31 of each year, including amounts paid, balance owed, and number of payments remaining.

The buyer's right to cancel within 14 days of signing is preserved by Texas Property Code §5.072(d).

3.TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure (TRID)

Ream provides Loan Estimate and Closing Disclosure documents in compliance with the Truth-in-Lending Act and Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act for all owner-financed transactions exceeding $25,000 secured by real property. The Loan Estimate is provided within 3 business days of application; the Closing Disclosure at least 3 business days before signing.

6.Anti-Money Laundering (AML)

As a financial institution under the Bank Secrecy Act, Ream maintains a written AML program, conducts customer due diligence on every applicant, files Currency Transaction Reports for cash transactions exceeding $10,000, and files Suspicious Activity Reports as required by 31 CFR §1029.320.

7.Mortgage Interest Reporting

Ream issues IRS Form 1098 (Mortgage Interest Statement) to each borrower by January 31 each year, reflecting interest paid on financed transactions secured by real property. Borrowers should consult a tax professional for guidance on deductibility.

8.Complaints & recovery

Buyers may file complaints with:

  • Texas Real Estate Commission — for brokerage conduct. trec.texas.gov
  • Texas Department of Savings and Mortgage Lending — for owner-financing complaints. sml.texas.gov
  • Consumer Financial Protection Bureau — federal complaints. consumerfinance.gov

The TREC Real Estate Recovery Trust Account may reimburse aggrieved persons up to $50,000 per transaction for damages caused by license-holder fraud — see TREC website for the application form.

Notice. Real-estate transactions and owner-financed loans involve substantial financial commitments. Ream encourages every buyer to engage independent legal counsel and a licensed Texas attorney before signing any agreement. Nothing on the Ream platform constitutes legal, tax, or financial advice.
