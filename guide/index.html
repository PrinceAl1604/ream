Everything you need to run real estate financing on REAM.

REAM is the operating system for owner-financed land and home sales — from listing a plot, to capturing prospects, to signing a loan, to collecting decades of monthly payments. This guide explains how it fits together, the conventions every team should know, and what each kind of user can do.

01 · OVERVIEW

What REAM is.

REAM is a vertical SaaS for organizations that sell real estate using their own money — pre-financed land, owner-financed homes, rent-to-own — and need to manage every step of that relationship for the next 5 to 30 years.

Most CRMs end at "deal closed". Most loan-servicing software starts at "loan booked". REAM joins them: a public listing site at one end, a structured loan ledger at the other, and a single timeline of activities, documents, and payments running between them. One organization, many sites, many properties, many customers, many years.

What you can do on the platform

  • List inventory — sites, individual plots, and finished houses, with public-facing detail pages.
  • Capture prospects — through a public intake form, an agent's manual entry, CSV import, or shared invite link.
  • Underwrite and offer — compute indicative offers, request documents, qualify or reject.
  • Sign and book a loan — generate a contract from templates, capture a wet/e-signature, book the loan.
  • Service the loan — log payments, reconcile bank statements, send reminders, calculate payoffs.
  • Stay in touch — log every call, email, SMS, WhatsApp, note, and payment on a single timeline per customer.
  • Run the team — invite agents, assign portfolios, set quotas, watch dashboards by role and tier.
02 · LIFECYCLE

How a customer flows through.

Every customer on REAM moves through five stages. Most pages in the app are scoped to one of them — knowing where someone is tells you which screens apply.

STAGE 1
Visitor
Anonymous

Browses listings on the public site. No account.

STAGE 2
Prospect
Applicant

Submitted an interest form. Has a portal to track it.

STAGE 3
Just signed
New customer

Contract executed. Onboarding checklist active.

STAGE 4
Active
Loan holder

Paying monthly. Statements, AutoPay, calculator.

STAGE 5
Paid off
Owner

Loan closed. Title transferred. Documents archived.

A single record carries through the whole lifecycle: the prospect record becomes the customer record, the property they applied for becomes the property on the loan, and the activity timeline accumulates across stages. You never lose history.

03 · VOCABULARY

Core concepts.

A handful of nouns appear everywhere in the app. Get fluent with these and most pages will read themselves.

SITE

Site

A development — an estate, an acreage, a township. The container for plots and houses. Has a map, a name, and a few aggregate stats.

LISTING

Listing

An individual unit for sale: a plot of land or a finished house. Belongs to a site. Has a price, terms, photos, and a public detail page.

PROSPECT

Prospect

A person interested in a listing. Captured from the public form, an import, or by an agent. Pipeline-stage, not yet a customer.

CUSTOMER

Customer

A prospect who has signed. Owns one or more loans and properties. Has a portal account.

LOAN

Loan

The financing contract for one property. Tracks principal, interest, schedule, balance, and every transaction against it.

ACTIVITY

Activity

Anything that happened on a relationship — note, call, email, SMS, WhatsApp, payment. All shown on one timeline. Note Call Email SMS WhatsApp Payment

DOC REQUEST

Document request

An ask for a specific file from a prospect or customer (ID, payslip, bank statement). Tracked separately so nothing slips.

TEMPLATE

Template

A reusable shape — for activities, document requests, emails, contracts. Defines the fields and copy your team uses.

ORG

Organization

The tenant. Your company. Has its own brand, listings, team, customers — fully isolated from other orgs on REAM.

04 · GROUND RULES

Conventions to know before you start.

A few patterns repeat throughout REAM. Learn them once and the rest of the app gets predictable.

One person, one record

A prospect record becomes the customer record on signing. Don't create a new account for the same person — find them and convert. The system de-duplicates by phone and email.

Everything goes on the timeline

If it happened, log it. Calls, "no answer", payments, even internal notes. The timeline is the source of truth for handoffs between agents and audits later.

Activities can have parents and children

Use "Sub-activity of…" on a follow-up call to link it to the original email. The parent shows a panel of children; the child shows a ribbon back to the parent.

Roles are scoped, not titles

Your dashboard, sidebar, and what you can edit all change with your role and tier (agent / manager / admin) — not with a job title. Ask your org admin for the right one.

Templates first, then transactions

Before sending the first email or generating the first contract, build the template. The same applies to activity types, doc requests, and loan products.

Payments are reviewed, not auto-applied

Logged or imported payments enter a review queue before they hit a loan ledger. Finance approves and allocates. This keeps the books reconcilable.

Invites, not signups

Internal users are added by invite from an admin — never by self-signup. Customers, by contrast, do self-sign-up against an existing application.

Public versus private detail pages

Listings have a public page (no PII, marketing copy) and an internal page (operational fields, owner data). Always verify which one a link points to before sharing externally.

05 · SETUP

Before you start using the app.

If your organization is brand-new on REAM, an admin should walk through these in order. Most can be done in an afternoon; templates and listings continue to grow over time.

1

Create your organization

Run the agency onboarding wizard: profile, location, legal entity, branding. This becomes the namespace for everything that follows. Only one organization per legal entity.

Org admin
2

Invite your team

Add agents, managers, and admins by email. Assign each one a line (sales / finance / follow-up) and a tier. They confirm via an emailed invite link.

Org admin
3

Set up loan products

Define your standard offer shapes — term, interest, deposit, fees. Every loan booked later picks one of these as a starting point.

Finance admin
4

Build templates

Activity types, email templates, document templates (the loan agreement), and standard document requests. Anything you'd rewrite twice belongs in a template.

Org admin
5

Add sites and listings

Create the site (the development), then add plots and houses underneath it. Upload photos and pricing. Public detail pages go live as soon as a listing is published.

Sales admin
6

Import existing prospects & customers

If you're migrating from a spreadsheet or another CRM, use the imports tool — CSV/Excel for prospects, customers, properties, and historic transactions. Map columns, preview, then commit.

Org admin
7

Connect the public site

Point your domain at the listings page, share the public intake link, and you're collecting prospects. Day-one operational state achieved.

Org admin

Don't skip step 4

Templates are easy to skip and brutal to retrofit. Once you've sent 200 emails or booked 50 loans, redoing the templates means migrating live data. Spend an hour up front.

06 · ROLES

What each user can do.

REAM has eight kinds of users. The first three are external (visitors, applicants, customers); the next four are your internal team, each with three tiers; the last is platform staff. Pages, dashboards, and permissions all change with the role.

External · No account

Public visitor

An anonymous person browsing your listings or marketing site. Top of the funnel. No login required.

Read-only

Can do

  • Browse the landing page and learn how financing works
  • Open the listings catalog, filter by site, price, size
  • View any land or house detail page — photos, terms, location
  • Submit the express-interest form to become a prospect
  • Sign in if they already have a customer account

Cannot do

  • See any internal pricing, owner details, or activity history
  • Communicate with an agent until they submit interest
  • Reserve, hold, or pay for a property without applying

Key pages

  • Landing · Browse listings · Asset detail · Express interest · Financing
External · Application in flight

Prospect

Someone who submitted the interest form. They have a portal account scoped to their application until they sign — then they become a customer.

Their record only

Can do

  • Track the stage of their application on a portal timeline
  • See the indicative offer once finance generates one
  • View, fulfil, and reply to document requests from the team
  • Upload IDs, payslips, bank statements, and any other ask
  • Continue browsing listings and saving favorites
  • Message their assigned sales agent

Cannot do

  • See any other prospect or customer's data
  • Sign a final loan contract — that happens after qualification
  • Make a payment until a loan is booked against them

Key pages

  • My application · Submission receipt · My requests · Request detail
External · Signed contract

Customer

A prospect who has signed a loan agreement. Has the full customer portal — and the portal adapts as they move through four lifecycle states.

Their portfolio only

Can do

  • See the next payment, schedule, balance, and history
  • Set up AutoPay on a card or bank account
  • Log a one-off payment and watch it clear review
  • Run the payoff calculator for early settlement scenarios
  • Download contracts, statements, receipts, and tax letters
  • Open a request to follow-up support and track its SLA
  • Manage multiple properties and AutoPay rules at once if they have several loans

Cannot do

  • Modify the loan contract, rate, or schedule directly
  • See internal team notes about their account

Lifecycle states

  • Just signed — onboarding checklist, AutoPay setup, first-payment countdown
  • Active — standard loan-holder dashboard
  • Multi-property — portfolio totals, per-property cards, bulk AutoPay
  • Paid off — archive view, title transfer documents
Internal team · 3 tiers

Sales

Owns the relationship from intake through signed contract: works the prospect pipeline, runs site visits, builds offers, and brings deals to close.

Pipeline + assigned

Can do

  • Work the prospects pipeline — kanban or table view
  • Open a prospect's full detail with timeline, documents, offer
  • Log calls, emails, SMS, WhatsApp, notes — link them as parent / sub-activity
  • Generate the indicative offer and share it with the prospect
  • Request documents using saved templates
  • Manage site & listing inventory — create and edit listings
  • Generate the loan agreement from a document template and walk the prospect through signing

Cannot do

  • Approve a payment or modify the loan ledger (finance does)
  • Invite or remove other internal users (org admin does)

Key pages

  • Prospects · Prospect detail · Sites · Houses · Lands · Loan create
AGENT
Sales agent
Owns a portfolio of prospects. Sees today's tasks, hot leads, quota progress.
MANAGER
Sales manager
Sees the whole team's funnel, leaderboard, forecast, coaching queue. Reassigns prospects.
ADMIN
Sales admin
Org-wide pipeline, lead-source mix, site demand, revenue-to-plan. Sets sales policies and templates.
Internal team · 3 tiers

Finance

Owns the loan ledger after signing: confirms payments, runs reconciliation, manages aging, and reports on portfolio health.

All loans

Can do

  • Work the payment review queue — approve, reject, allocate
  • Open any loan detail (admin) — schedule, balance, transactions
  • Run the payoff calculator for early-settlement scenarios
  • Import a bank statement and reconcile against expected receipts
  • Edit loan products — terms, rates, fees
  • See aging buckets, NPL, IFRS-9 provisions, vintage cohorts
  • Verify uploaded documents (IDs, bank letters)

Cannot do

  • Edit the prospect pipeline or sign a contract (sales does)
  • Re-issue a contract template — that's an org-admin action

Key pages

  • Loans · Loan detail · Repayments · Payment review · Reports · Doc verify
AGENT
Finance agent
Verifications queue, today's reminders, past-due workload, payment confirmations.
MANAGER
Finance manager
Aging buckets, team workload, high-risk accounts, day-end reconciliation.
ADMIN
Finance admin
Portfolio aging, NPL, provisions, vintage cohorts, compliance reports. Defines loan products.
Internal team · 3 tiers

Follow-up

Owns the post-signing relationship: customer requests, retention, payment-difficulty conversations, satisfaction. The "customer success" line for a 30-year product.

Assigned customers

Can do

  • Work a queue of customer requests with SLA timers
  • Open any active customer's full activity timeline
  • Log calls, SMS, WhatsApp; create payment-promise activities
  • Send templated emails (statements, reminders, payoff quotes)
  • Schedule follow-up tasks as sub-activities of an original call
  • Flag at-risk accounts and hand off to finance

Cannot do

  • Approve a payment (finance reviews) or change loan terms
  • Edit the prospect pipeline (sales does)

Key pages

  • Customers · Customer detail · Document requests · Activity
AGENT
Follow-up agent
My customers, today's check-ins, open requests, SLA timers, recent conversations.
MANAGER
Follow-up manager
Team SLA, CSAT, request mix, coaching items, at-risk accounts.
ADMIN
Follow-up admin
Customer journey health, retention, NPS, channel mix, themes, playbooks, QA.
Internal · Org owner

Organization admin

The person who owns the REAM tenant. Sets up the org, manages every internal user, defines templates, owns billing and policies.

Everything in the org

Can do

  • Run the onboarding wizard and edit org settings, branding, legal entity
  • Invite, promote, demote, or remove any internal user
  • Edit every template — activity, document request, email, contract
  • Run imports — bulk-load prospects, customers, properties, transactions
  • See every dashboard for sales, finance, and follow-up at the admin tier
  • Read the full activity log for compliance and audit

Cannot do

  • Touch another organization's data — strict tenant isolation
  • Modify platform-level feature flags (super admin does)

Key pages

  • Org settings · Agents / Managers / Admins · Templates · Imports · Activity log
Platform · REAM staff only

Super admin

REAM platform staff. Manages organizations, runs the platform, supports customers. Not part of any single tenant — sits above all of them.

All organizations

Can do

  • See and search every organization on REAM
  • Search any user across any org
  • Act as an org admin to debug a customer's setup (audited)
  • Manage feature flags — beta access, kill switches, plan gating
  • Send platform broadcasts to one or all orgs
  • Read the audit log — every privileged action is recorded
  • See system health — uptime, queue depth, error rate

Operating principle

  • Default mode is read-only. Mutations require an Act-as session.
  • Every action is logged — orgs can audit super-admin access against their tenant.
  • Customer data is treated as confidential and only opened on a documented support ticket.

Key pages

  • Admin dashboard · Organizations · Users · Invites · Flags · Audit log · Broadcasts · Health
07 · SUPPORT

Getting help.

Stuck on a screen? Three places to look, in order.

  1. The page itself — most screens have an "i" icon next to the title with inline help.
  2. This guide — bookmark it. Sections 03 and 06 cover most "what does this mean" questions.
  3. Your org admin — for permissions, missing pages, or "I should be able to see this".

For platform-level issues — outages, suspected bugs, billing — your org admin can escalate to REAM support, who will respond from the platform's super-admin console.

One last thing

This guide is versioned alongside the app. When something major changes — a new lifecycle stage, a new role, a new module — this page is the first place it gets documented. Check the date in the header to see when you last looked.
